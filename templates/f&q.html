What is diff.blog?

You can think of diff.blog as a Medium.com alternative for developers. The difference between Medium and diff.blog is

  • diff.blog don't host your blog. You can publish your blog anywhere you want. It can be in your own Wordpress site or in a Jekyll site hosted in GitHub. You can even put your blog on Medium.com. No restrictions as long as it has an RSS feed.
  • Most developer blogs hosted outside of Medium.com are having a hard time finding a large audience. Diff.blog's was built to give visibility to these developer blogs that are spread across all over the Internet. It also has some cool features that makes it easier to subscribe to blogs.

Sounds great. How do you fetch posts from these blogs?

All most all the blogs have a RSS feed. RSS feeds makes it really easy to keep updated with the posts of a blog. No blockchain. Nothing fancy. It's just a file. You can read more about RSS feeds at https://www.youneedfeeds.com/.

Okay. So this is basically a RSS reader?

Kind of but not really. It has a lot of features that are not present in a normal RSS reader that would should give you better experience. Some of them are.

  • User recommendations - diff.blog can recommend you blogs to follow depending upon your interests. If you are interested in Rust for example diff.blog will suggest you awesome developers that blogs about Rust. When you sign up with your GitHub account diff.blog analyzes your profile to get a sense of what kind of topics you are interested in. Plus it also ask you to choose come more general topics when you sign up.
  • Auto follow - diff.blog automatically make you follow the blogs of developers and organizations you follow on GitHub. If you don't like some of them you can later unsubscribe as well.
  • Normal RSS readers knows only about blogs you follow. This might lead you to miss out on a lot of amazing content. diff.blog has a top feed that contains the popular blog posts from all over diff.blog.
  • Likes and comments - You can like blog posts and comment on them. The likes and comments are visible to other users of diff.blog.
  • Diff.blog has a Pocket integration. You can send articles you like to your Pocket account. There is even an option to send blog posts to your Pocket account automatically when someone you follow publishes a blog post. More integrations coming soon.

How do I add my blog?

You can add your blog by going to settings page after signup.

What if some blogs I want to follow are not there in diff.blog?

You can suggest the blog here and we would manually add them. Currently most of the featured blogs in diff.blog are added like that.

Okay. How much I have to pay to use this?

diff.blog is completely free to use. diff.blog is run on a $10 Digital Ocean droplet. It's currently my side project and cost me hardly any money to run.
